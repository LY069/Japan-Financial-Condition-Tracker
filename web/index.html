Methodology

Each indicator is converted to an accommodation score — a z-score against its own history since 2005, with the sign oriented so that a higher score always means more accommodative financial conditions (polarity-adjusted). Scores are aggregated into axes, two stages, and a single composite FCI.

≥ +0.15 accommodative −0.15 … +0.15 broadly neutral < −0.15 restrictive

Direction (▲ easing / ▼ tightening / → stable) is the trailing 6-month change in each accommodation score — i.e. whether a signal is turning more easy or more restrictive, shown alongside its level. The natural-rate panel adds a level-based second read: where the real policy rate sits inside the six-model band.

Indicator weights & assumptions

Each axis score is a weight-weighted mean of its member accommodation scores; the composite FCI is the equal-weight mean of the five axes. Weights follow the BoJ framework — short/medium real rates and the core funding-environment gauges carry full weight (1.0); longer-horizon or secondary gauges are down-weighted (0.5–0.75) because they have a relatively smaller direct impact on near-term activity. Polarity orients each series so a higher score always means more accommodative.
